Writing Rule Violations

Checked against: define by what things ARE (no negation framing), forbidden words, "but" usage.

1 Negation framing: "the inability to satisfy vital needs independently" MEDIUM
Location: Paragraph 7 (mentoring section)
Current: "the inability to satisfy vital needs independently, dressed as achievement"
Problem: Defines the mechanism by what it cannot do. The writing rule says: describe what things ARE.
Fix: "the dependence on others to satisfy vital needs, dressed as achievement"
2 Negation framing: "there is no motivation" LOW
Location: Paragraph 5 (golden cage explanation)
Current: "precisely because it satisfies, there is no motivation to develop the ability to satisfy those needs independently"
Problem: Defines state by the absence of motivation. Borderline — "no" is permitted per the rule, but the framing is still negative.
Fix: "precisely because it satisfies, the motivation to develop independent capacity vanishes"
3 Negation framing: "I cannot walk away from" LOW
Location: Paragraph 8 (quoted description)
Current: "I have built something I cannot walk away from"
Problem: "Cannot" is explicitly permitted by the rule. However, this is a character quote illustrating how people describe their condition — it represents their framing, which is inherently negative (the cage). This is acceptable as dialogue.
Verdict: No fix needed. The quote represents the caged mindset accurately.

Accuracy Notes

Claims that are simplified or could be more precise relative to the canon.

1 Unconscious obedience presented as conscious choice MEDIUM
Location: Paragraph 5
Current: "We follow the rules of the structure because our survival depends on it."
Canon (Law 123): "Reliance on the matnia for survival leads to unconscious obedience to its directives."
Problem: "We follow the rules" implies conscious, deliberate compliance. The canon specifies the obedience is unconscious — we obey without realizing we are obeying.
Fix: "We obey the directives of the structure without realizing it, because our survival depends on it."
2 Resolution mechanism incomplete LOW
Location: Paragraph 10 (resolution)
Current: "The cage opens when the dependency is identified and the capacity for self-reliance is rebuilt."
Canon (Laws 97, 125, 126): Healing requires releasing blocked emotional charges, feeling all emotions connected to the experience, and allowing the system to update and adapt.
Problem: The narrative implies identification + rebuilding is sufficient. The canon specifies that the underlying emotional charges must be released — identification alone does not heal. This is a simplification, acceptable for a narrative but worth noting.
Verdict: Acceptable for narrative tone. The mentoring context implies the deeper work happens in sessions. Consider adding one sentence about releasing the emotional charges beneath the dependency.
