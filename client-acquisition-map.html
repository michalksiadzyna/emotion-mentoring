Acquisition Paths

Six Clients, Six Paths

Each client acquired through a different channel at a different velocity. Ordered by expected acquisition speed.

Week 1-3
Client 1
Direct Outreach — Warm Network
Identify 10-20 warm contacts → Personal message (genuine, not salesy) → Conversation about their situation → Offer free assessment → Close on the call
What you do: Identify people who already know your work, have seen transformations, or expressed curiosity. Reach out personally. Three to five messages per day until you have 2-3 assessments booked.
Why this works first: Zero friction. They already trust you. Conversion rate is 50%+ on warm leads.
Prerequisite: A list of contacts. People from past sessions, friends who have witnessed results, anyone who has said "I need something like that."
Week 2-4
Client 2
Referral — Existing Contacts Introduce Someone
Ask 5-10 people for referrals → They introduce someone specific → Warm intro conversation → Assessment call → Close
What you do: Ask people who know your work: "Do you know anyone struggling with [specific pattern]?" Give them a concrete description, not a vague ask. Make it easy to think of one person.
Why this works second: Referred prospects arrive with pre-built trust. Close rate 40-50%.
Prerequisite: Client 1 outreach is already happening. Same contact list, different ask.
Week 4-8
Client 3
LinkedIn — Knifehead Content + GLASS Engagement
Daily LinkedIn posts (Knifehead) → GLASS drops comments on target profiles → Prospect visits your profile → Clicks emotionmentoring.com → Books assessment → Close
What you do: Activate Knifehead Phase 2 content production. One LinkedIn post per weekday. Activate GLASS to find and engage with ideal prospects via thoughtful comments. The content builds authority; GLASS puts you in front of the right people.
Why LinkedIn third: Professional audience, higher intent, longer content format works well for the Shen Method depth. Takes 3-4 weeks of consistent posting before the algorithm surfaces you reliably.
Prerequisites: Knifehead Phase 2 running. GLASS Phase 1 complete (database + discovery). LinkedIn profile already optimized (done 2026-03-10).
Week 6-10
Client 4
Instagram — Carousels + Stories + GLASS
Regular carousels on @shenfreelife → GLASS engages on emotional wellness accounts → Prospect follows, watches stories → Multiple touchpoints over days → Link in bio → website → Assessment → close
What you do: Produce 2 feed posts + 5 stories per day (Knifehead). GLASS runs engagement on Instagram emotional wellness space. The path is longer here — Instagram prospects need more touchpoints before they click the bio link.
Why Instagram fourth: Higher volume, lower individual intent. Requires multiple exposures. The carousel format showcases Shen Method laws beautifully, but conversion takes longer than LinkedIn.
Prerequisites: Knifehead carousel templates working (first carousel done). GLASS active on Instagram. Compelling link-in-bio setup.
Month 2-4
Client 5
Referral — From Client 1 or 2
Deliver results to early clients → Ask for referral at natural moment → Warm intro → Assessment → Close
What you do: After 3-4 sessions with Client 1 or 2, when they experience a breakthrough, ask: "Do you know someone who is dealing with something similar?" Timing matters — ask when they feel the value, not before.
Why this works fifth: Your best marketing is a transformed client talking about it. Referrals from paying clients close at the highest rate of any channel.
Prerequisites: Client 1 and 2 already working with you. Genuine results delivered. This is organic — it happens because the work is real.
Month 3-4
Client 6
Content Convert — Cross-Platform Compounding
Content library reaches critical mass → Prospect discovers via any platform → Cross-checks on other platforms (social proof) → Reads website + FAQs → Books assessment → Close
What you do: By month 3, you have 60+ LinkedIn posts, 100+ Instagram pieces, active GLASS engagement, and a growing digital footprint. The compounding effect means people find you from multiple angles. Someone sees a LinkedIn post, checks Instagram, reads the website, and books.
Why this works last: Compounding requires time and volume. This client does not come from any single action — they come from the accumulated weight of consistent presence across platforms.
Prerequisites: 8-12 weeks of consistent content output. All platforms active. Website polished and converting.

Critical Path

What Blocks This Plan

Remove these or the timeline extends.

Human-Only Blockers
  • Warm contact list: Only you know who has seen your work and who might be open to a conversation. No automation replaces this. Build the list this week.
  • Assessment calls: Only you can run the 45-minute sessions and close. This is the bottleneck — every assessment you do not take is a client you do not get.
  • Referral asks: Only you can make the personal ask. A message from you carries weight that no automation can replicate.
Machine Blockers (Claude Handles)
  • GLASS Phase 1 (database + discovery): Engagement engine requires setup before it can start warming prospects. Target: operational this week.
  • Knifehead Phase 2 (content production): Daily content across platforms. Templates exist. Narrative pipeline queued. Target: daily output starting this week.
  • Form submission bug (FIXPLAN 3.1): Silent false-success on form submission means you are losing assessments right now. Critical fix — prioritize before anything else on the website.
The Honest Math
If you send 3 personal messages per day to warm contacts for 2 weeks, that is 30-40 conversations. At a 50% assessment booking rate, that is 15-20 assessments. At a 50% close rate on warm leads, that is 7-10 clients from outreach alone.

The content engine (Knifehead + GLASS) is the long game — it builds the pipeline that sustains the business after warm contacts are exhausted. But the first 2-3 clients come from picking up the phone.
